01 · Problem

Problem

Applying for car insurance or a personal loan takes minutes online. The process is slick, guided, and entirely self-service. Yet in the UK legal sector, onboarding a new client for a road traffic accident claim or an employment dispute still typically involves a phone call to a contact centre, a manual script, and a handler typing data into a legacy system. The legal industry is decades behind adjacent sectors like finance and insurance when it comes to client intake — and it's costing firms millions.

Most UK law firms still rely on Contact Centres for B2C case intake — call handlers who manually capture client data using scripts over the phone and raise cases in their Case Management System (CMS). These legacy CMS platforms were designed decades ago for basic case tracking, not for complex, multi-step client onboarding workflows, making the intake process inherently inefficient and error-prone. The result is a poor experience for both the firm and the client.

The Hidden Cost of Manual Client Intake

Direct staffing costs

Call handlers earn £25k–£35k annually, with an average of £27.5k across junior and senior staff. Mid-size firms like Thompsons Scotland operate with around 30 FTE contact centre staff, representing £825k in annual staffing costs before overheads, training, and management time. Digital intake systems can reduce contact centre headcount from 30 to 5 staff (83% reduction), potentially saving £687.5k annually in staffing costs alone.

Error amplification costs

Legacy CMS systems force call handlers into inefficient data capture workflows, creating a cascade of expensive problems:

  • Clunky, outdated CMS interfaces make it easy for call handlers to enter data incorrectly during calls
  • Incorrect client details require fee earners to spend billable time correcting information
  • With solicitor hourly rates ranging from £190–£490+ depending on experience and location, even 30 minutes of correction time per error costs £95–£245 in lost billable time
  • Incomplete initial capture forces multiple lawyer follow-up calls, increasing time investment and cost

Capacity bottleneck

Contact centres become a constraint on growth, as firms must hire and train additional staff to handle increased enquiry volumes, creating a linear cost scaling problem.

The Client Experience Gap

The cost and efficiency problems above are compounded by a poor experience for the end-user — the person making a claim or enquiring about legal services. Today's clients expect the same seamless, digital-first experience they get when applying for insurance or opening a bank account. Instead, they get phone queues during office hours, repeated requests for the same information, and no visibility into what happens after the call ends. This gap between expectation and reality leads to lost enquiries, poor first impressions, and lower client satisfaction from day one.

Instructd closes this gap by giving end-users a modern, self-service onboarding experience they can complete at any time, on any device, with instant confirmation and — through the client portal — ongoing visibility into their case progress, real-time notifications, and easy asynchronous communication with their firm.

Market Timing: Why This Matters Now

  • Increased competition: Getting left behind as other firms adopt modern technology
  • Regulatory changes: That cap fees — this means efficiency gains are paramount
  • Talent shortage: Admin staff are easy to hire, but hard to train and retain
  • Client expectations: Clients increasingly expect 24/7 self-service options like other industries

The legal sector needs a solution that eliminates the call handler bottleneck entirely, allowing clients to provide complete, validated information directly into case management systems without human intervention — turning a cost centre into a competitive advantage. This is a standard approach in other sectors e.g. financial services & insurance so the idea is validated in adjacent sectors. While adjacent solutions exist in the legal tech market (see Competitive Landscape), no competitor offers a purpose-built, low-code platform specifically designed for complex legal intake workflows with the depth of customisation, white-labelling, and consumption-based pricing that Instructd provides.

This urgent need is validated by our first customer — a UK law firm going live on the Instructd platform in March 2026, demonstrating real market demand for this solution.

Product Expansion

Self-service client portals

Client portals represent a natural and high-value expansion of the Instructd platform, extending its reach beyond initial intake into ongoing client management. The concept is familiar to anyone who has applied for a mortgage: after completing the application, you log into a portal to track progress, upload documents, receive updates, and message your advisor. Legal clients expect this same experience but rarely get it.

At the end of a completed onboarding journey, end-users are prompted to create an account and access a white-labelled client portal for their ongoing case. This creates a seamless transition from intake to case management.

Core Portal Features:

  • Case status tracking and notifications — Real-time updates on case progress, key milestones, and next steps, delivered via email, SMS, or in-app notifications, eliminating the "Where's my case?" phone calls that consume admin time
  • Two-way secure messaging — Structured, compliant communication channels between client and firm, replacing email and phone tag with an auditable, organised messaging system
  • Document uploads and requests — Clients can securely submit required documents and the firm can request specific documents with deadlines, removing the back-and-forth of chasing paperwork
  • Fee transparency and payment processing — Clients can view invoices, understand billing, and make payments directly through the portal

Strategic Value:

The client portal transforms Instructd from a point-of-intake tool into an ongoing client engagement platform. This significantly increases the value per customer, creates additional consumption-based revenue opportunities (e.g. per-notification or per-message pricing), and makes Instructd deeply embedded in the firm's client-facing operations — dramatically reducing churn risk.

Multiple prospective clients have already expressed interest in this capability during early sales conversations, validating the demand.

Industry and sector expansion

Once traction is established in UK legal, the same platform can be offered to global legal markets and adjacent regulated industries. 06 · Revenue Model & Pricing Strategy

Revenue Model & Pricing Strategy

Instructd uses a consumption-based pricing model — charging per successful journey completion. This directly aligns our revenue with the value we deliver: firms only pay when a client successfully completes an onboarding journey, replacing what would otherwise be a costly manual intake call.

Strategic Pricing Philosophy

Consumption pricing, justified by:

  • Zero-risk adoption: Firms pay nothing until the platform delivers value through completed journeys
  • Aligned incentives: Instructd is financially motivated to maximise journey completion rates, directly benefiting the client
  • Scalable revenue: Revenue grows naturally as firms expand to more case types and drive more volume through the platform
  • Simple ROI calculation: "If we process 10,000 completions at £X, our cost is £Y vs £Z for contact centre staff"

This model is already familiar to legal firms through existing per-transaction services, and Instructd's £3 per completion is compelling against every comparable cost a firm already incurs:

Value Anchors to Justify Pricing

Per Completion ROI Calculation:

A contact centre handler on a £30k salary working a 37.5-hour week costs approximately £3.08 per case in direct staff cost alone — and that assumes 5 cases per hour, which is optimistic for complex case types. At £3 per completion, Instructd is already cheaper than the direct staffing cost before accounting for any of the additional savings:

  • Elimination of errors: Fee earner correction time at £95–£245+ per error (solicitor hourly rates of £190–£490+)

  • 24/7 availability: Digital intake captures enquiries outside office hours that would otherwise be missed entirely
  • Staff cost reduction: Training, recruitment, and retention costs for high-turnover contact centre roles are eliminated
  • Automated downstream processing: Higher quality structured data opens the route for automated case acceptance and valuation

At £3 per completion, Instructd is not just cheaper than the alternative — it fundamentally changes the economics of client intake by removing the bottleneck that limits how many cases a firm can process.

09 · Strategic Advantages

Strategic Advantages

Data Moat & Intellectual Property

Instructd's platform generates two categories of valuable intellectual property that compound over time:

Proprietary Technology:

  • Journey Schema DSL — A domain-specific language for defining complex, multi-step onboarding workflows with branching logic, validation rules, and integration points. This is a genuine technical innovation purpose-built for regulated intake workflows.
  • Plugin Architecture — An extensible framework allowing custom Typescript-based integrations, creating an ecosystem around the platform.
  • Secure Multi-Tenant Architecture — A defence-in-depth infrastructure approach with strict tenant isolation, private network endpoints, and automated security testing that blocks deployment if isolation controls fail. Clients authenticate via their own Entra (Azure AD) tenant, retaining full control of their identity security policies.

Data Assets:

Every journey completion generates structured, validated data. As the platform scales, this data will flow into a dedicated data lake (see Product Roadmap, Phase 4) to power the machine learning optimisation features. Over time, this creates an increasingly valuable dataset and a significant competitive barrier. No new entrant can replicate years of accumulated intake data across multiple firms and case types.

Market Dynamics & Customer Retention

Law firms are characteristically resistant to change. While this creates a longer initial sales cycle, it produces exceptionally strong customer retention once a firm is onboarded:

  • High switching costs: Once a firm's intake workflows are built on Instructd, migrating to an alternative would require rebuilding every journey, retraining staff, and re-establishing integrations — a cost and disruption that firms will avoid
  • Deep operational embedding: As firms add more case types and the client portal, Instructd becomes deeply integrated into daily operations
  • Long-tail recurring revenue: Each customer relationship, once established, generates reliable consumption revenue with minimal ongoing sales effort, and grows organically as firms expand usage

Additionally, every law firm has different business processes and will not change them to fit a platform. This is precisely why Instructd's highly configurable, low-code approach works — the platform adapts to the firm's processes rather than requiring the firm to adapt. This level of flexibility is extremely difficult for generic tools to replicate and validates the premium, high-value commercial model.

AI & Machine Learning Strategy

Instructd's journey schema and the structured data it captures create a powerful foundation for AI and machine learning capabilities. Rather than treating AI as a bolt-on feature, we are building it into the platform at three distinct levels: enhancing the end-user experience, accelerating journey creation for our clients, and using data intelligence to continuously improve outcomes.

1. Client-Facing AI Chatbot

An AI chatbot that sits on top of the existing journey schema, providing an alternative interface for end-users who prefer conversational interaction over traditional form-filling. The chatbot uses the journey's validation rules, branching logic, and field definitions as its context, ensuring it collects exactly the same structured data as the form-based journey while providing a more natural experience. This maintains all of Instructd's data quality and compliance benefits while offering firms a modern, accessible intake channel.

2. Generative AI for Journey Creation

AI-assisted content generation within the journey builder, allowing our clients' teams to rapidly create professional HTML content for their onboarding journeys. This includes generating introduction pages, explanatory content between steps, confirmation pages, and legal disclaimers — all tailored to the firm's tone of voice and branding. This dramatically reduces the time and skill required to build polished, client-facing journeys, making the platform accessible to non-technical staff.

3. Machine Learning for Journey Optimisation

Instructd captures large volumes of structured end-user onboarding data across all customers' journeys. Over time, this data lake becomes a significant strategic asset. By applying machine learning to cross-reference completion rates, drop-off points, and data quality across journeys, we can automatically identify patterns in high-performing journeys and generate actionable recommendations for improvement. For example: suggesting question reordering, identifying fields that cause drop-offs, or recommending content changes that improve completion rates. This positions Instructd as an intelligent platform that gets smarter with every journey completion.

4. AI Agent Integration & Quality Gate

As AI agents become more prevalent in legal workflows, Instructd is positioned to serve as the validation and quality control layer between AI systems and case management systems. AI agents lack built-in validation, quality gates, and compliance controls — Instructd provides these. By supporting hybrid workflows where AI technologies are layered on top of Instructd's structured journey framework, firms can adopt AI confidently without risk of data pollution or compliance failures. This transforms AI agents from a competitive threat into a complementary technology that enhances Instructd's value proposition.
