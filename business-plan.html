01 · Problem

Legal intake is
stuck in the past.

Applying for car insurance takes minutes online. Onboarding a new legal client still means a phone queue, a call handler, and a form typed into a legacy system. The legal industry is decades behind adjacent sectors — and it's costing firms millions.

£825k
Annual staffing cost for a mid-size firm's contact centre — 30 FTE at avg. £27.5k, before overheads
83%
Potential headcount reduction: from 30 contact centre staff down to 5 with digital intake
£245
Cost per correction error in fee earner time — at £490/hr senior solicitor rates, 30 mins per error

Four critical pain points

High staffing costs

Call handlers earn £25–35k annually. Mid-size firms operate with around 30 FTE contact centre staff representing £825k in annual costs before overheads, training, and management time.

Error-prone data capture

Clunky, outdated CMS interfaces make it easy for call handlers to enter data incorrectly during calls. Fee earners at £190–490/hr then spend billable time correcting avoidable mistakes.

Capacity bottleneck

Contact centres create a linear cost scaling problem. Growth means hiring more staff. Intake only functions during staffed hours — missing out-of-hours enquiries entirely.

Poor client experience

Clients expect the same self-service experience they get from insurance or banking. Instead they get phone queues, repeated data requests, and zero visibility after the call ends.

The legal sector needs a solution that eliminates the call handler bottleneck entirely — allowing clients to provide complete, validated information directly into case management systems without human intervention. This is already standard in financial services and insurance. The model is validated. It simply hasn't been purpose-built for legal — until now.

Why now — converging market forces

Increased Competition
Firms adopting digital intake are winning enquiries that competitors miss. Digital-first intake is becoming a differentiator in contested practice areas.
Regulatory Pressure
Fee caps in personal injury and other areas mean efficiency gains are no longer optional — they are essential to maintaining margin.
Talent Shortage
High turnover in £25–35k contact centre roles creates continuous recruitment costs. Firms cannot reliably scale headcount to match enquiry volume.
Rising Client Expectations
Clients increasingly expect 24/7 digital self-service. Firms that deliver it see better conversion and stronger first impressions from day one.

03
PPC cost per lead (£200–500+) — This figure is based on US market data. A UK-specific source for legal PPC cost per lead / cost per acquisition is required to make this comparison credible for a UK audience.
04
Thompsons Scotland reference — The Problem section names Thompsons Scotland and cites internal staffing figures (30 FTE, £825k annual cost). Permission is required from Thompsons before including their name and operational data in any externally shared version of this plan.
05
Consumption pricing model is untested — No customer has yet agreed to pay-per-completion pricing. The first customer is on a flat-rate pilot. The consumption model assumptions should be validated as early as possible with prospective customers.
06
Ben's role and commitment — Ben's fractional involvement and potential transition to full-time needs clarifying as the business develops. The current plan reflects the arrangement as it stands today.
07
Contact centre headcount reduction claim — The claim that digital intake can reduce contact centre headcount from 30 to 5 staff (83% reduction) needs concrete evidence. Specifically: how many call handlers would a firm like Thompsons need if all B2C cases were digitised through Instructd? This should be validated with real data from the pilot customer.

Solicitor hourly rates — The claim that solicitor rates range from £190–£490+ needs a credible source (e.g. Law Society rate guidelines or published legal industry salary data).
