Low-code client intake for UK law firms

Instructd gives firms total control over onboarding and data-capture journeys — without permanent dev teams or six-month build cycles. White-label, CMS-integrated, built for B2C legal volume.

01
02

The problem

Insurance takes minutes online. Many law firms still open files on a phone call.

B2C legal intake is still contact-centre and script driven: handlers transcribe into legacy case management, errors cascade to fee earners, and capacity scales only with more headcount.

  • Staffing: large-volume firms run big contact centre teams; growth means linear hiring and churn.
  • Data quality: manual capture wastes billable time fixing mistakes and chasing incomplete information.
  • Client expectation: people expect 24/7, self-serve, guided journeys like every other industry.
03

Why now

Efficiency, fees, and competition are squeezing the old model

Market

Competition for consumer legal work is higher; clients compare you to digital-native experiences. Fee pressure in parts of the market makes unit cost in intake more visible.

Tech

Case management and integration maturity mean structured intake can finally land in the file — if the journey layer is built for legal complexity, not a generic form.

04

Solution

Instructd

A low-code platform for client onboarding: business teams own the journey, IT keeps guardrails, and completed journeys push structured data into the CMS.

  • Visual editor — complex branching, validation, and publishing in days, not multi-quarter project cycles.
  • White-label — your domain, your brand; the client does not see a third-party app logo.
  • Integrations — webhooks, plugins (identity, sign, address, etc.), and deep hooks into platforms such as Sharedo in the UK ecosystem.
  • Safe deployment — dev / test / live, version history, rollback; Entra (Azure AD) for firm-controlled identity and MFA.
05

Who it’s for

Medium to large UK firms, B2C volume work

Ideal customers run multiple concurrent intake workflows, have innovation or legal ops capacity, and want to grow file intake without scaling contact-centre FTE in lockstep.

Strong fit — high-volume, process-first matter types: RTA, employment triage, personal injury, group litigation, campaign-driven sign-up.
