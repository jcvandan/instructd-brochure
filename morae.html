01 Pitch · 2026

We're rebuilding how UK law firms open client files.

For a decade, insurance and banking have onboarded customers online. Legal stuck with the phone. Instructd is the platform changing that - across self-serve client journeys, a call-handler intake UI, and a post-intake client portal. One platform, one schema, three ways into a law firm.

02 The problem

Insurance takes minutes online. Many law firms still open files on a phone call.

B2C legal intake is still contact-centre and script-driven - handlers transcribe into legacy case management, errors cascade to fee earners, and capacity scales only with more headcount.

  • Staffing: large-volume firms run big contact-centre teams; growth means linear hiring and churn.
  • Data quality: manual capture wastes billable time fixing mistakes and chasing missing information.
  • Client expectation: people expect 24/7, self-serve, guided journeys like every other industry.
03 Why now

Efficiency, fees, and competition are squeezing the old model

Market

Competition for consumer legal work is higher; clients compare law firms to digital-native experiences. Fee pressure makes the unit cost of intake more visible.

Tech

Case management maturity means structured intake can finally land directly in the file - if the journey layer is built for legal complexity, not a generic web form.

Engineering stance

Security & scaling, distilled

The full security white paper is available under NDA for any technical buyer that asks.

Edge protection

Cloudflare in front of every domain - including each firm's own branded domain. Automatic DDoS mitigation (L3/4 + L7), WAF rules, bot detection, rate limiting, managed SSL/TLS certificates. TLS 1.2+ enforced.

Network isolation

All data services (Cosmos DB, Key Vault, Storage) sit inside a private Azure Virtual Network behind Private Endpoints. Public network access is disabled entirely on the data tier - there is no internet-routable path to client data.

Tenant isolation

Application-level row-level security. Every database query is automatically filtered by organisation ID, which is derived from the domain or a signed JWT - never from user input. Automated end-to-end tests prove isolation on every deployment; deployment is blocked if isolation tests fail.

Encryption & assurance

AES-256 at rest across Cosmos, Storage, and Key Vault (HSM-backed, FIPS 140-2 L2). TLS 1.2+ in transit, end-to-end. Cyber Essentials Plus in progress; ISO 27001 alignment targeted for Q2 2026. Continuous monitoring via Microsoft Defender for Cloud.

High-level path. Public attackers can't reach the data tier directly - only the Cloudflare-fronted app tier is internet-routable, and every query through it is automatically filtered by tenant.

How far it scales

Honest, defensible capacity numbers - based on the current production stack (Azure App Service Plan P1v4, Cosmos DB serverless, Cloudflare-fronted). No marketing inflation.

Today
Single production app service plan instance, Cosmos serverless, all firms multi-tenanted on the same hardened platform. Static assets cached at the Cloudflare edge so journey starts are sub-second globally.
~1,000 concurrent
Near-term
Horizontal scale on the same App Service Plan to 10–20 instances. Zero code change, zero architectural change. Routine because the app tier is stateless and session-less.
~10,000–20,000 concurrent
Medium-term
When any single firm pushes past ~50k completions/year, migrate Cosmos from serverless to autoscale provisioned throughput (up to 1M RU/s per container) and step the plan to P2v4 / P3v4. Straight-line scale.
50,000+ concurrent
Geo & DR
All data hosted in UK South across multiple availability zones. Cross-region replication available on request. RTO 1–4 hours; RPO < 1 hour. Point-in-time restore on Cosmos.
UK South + AZ-redundant

The bottom line for Morae: we are not infrastructure-constrained. Every firm Morae signs sits on the same hardened, multi-tenant platform - the only thing that scales linearly with growth is consumption cost, which the firm itself is paying for through the pricing.

Commercials

Two ways Morae can make money on this

Both options are revenue-share against the firm-paid revenue. The percentage, term length, exclusivity, and any minimum volumes are explicitly open for discussion - we'd rather agree the shape first and negotiate the numbers together.

Morae handles sales. Instructd handles everything else. Delivery, implementation, integrations, support, infrastructure, SLAs, security - all on us. Morae doesn't need to build delivery capability or take on operational risk. You bring the relationship, we bring the platform.

Option A
Consumption-based

Per completed enquiry

£3 per completed B2C enquiry
Billed monthly in arrears. No floor, no commitment.
Worked example. A mid-size firm running ~10,000 enquiries/year = £30,000/year per firm to Instructd. Morae earns a recurring % rev-share on every completion, for the life of the contract.
Why this works
  • Zero-risk adoption for the firm - they only pay when the platform delivers a completed intake.
  • Volume-aligned upside for Morae - the more the firm grows, the more Morae earns, with no cap.
  • Easy CFO conversation - direct comparison to per-call contact-centre cost.

Best fit when: the firm wants pure usage-based economics with no annual commitment.

Option B
Subscription

Annual contract per journey

£10k first journey · per year
+ £5,000/year for each additional journey on the platform.
Worked example. A firm running RTA + Employment + PI = £10k + £5k + £5k = £20,000/year per firm to Instructd. Morae earns a recurring % rev-share on the annual contract value.
Why this works
  • Predictable budgeting for the firm - flat annual line item, no surprises.
  • Easier procurement - annual SaaS contract is a familiar buying motion for legal IT.
  • Higher confidence revenue for Morae - committed annual base, regardless of volume.

Best fit when: the firm prefers fixed-fee predictability over volume-based pricing.

  • Contract term length

  • Mixed models per account
